Video Game Sales and Metacritic Scores

1. Introduction

Video games have become a dominant force in the entertainment industry, attracting millions of players worldwide. Over the years, the gaming market has expanded, with developers constantly pushing the boundaries of storytelling, gameplay mechanics, and visual fidelity. As the industry grows, consumers and analysts alike seek ways to measure the success of video games. While sales figures provide a clear financial perspective, review scores offer insight into critical reception. Together, these metrics help paint a picture of a game's overall impact. Metacritic is one of the most widely recognized review aggregation platforms for video games. It compiles scores from various critics and publications, generating an overall rating that serves as a benchmark for quality. High Metacritic scores can boost a game's reputation and influence consumer purchasing decisions, while lower scores may deter potential buyers. This study explores the relationship between video game sales and Metacritic scores, examining whether critical acclaim translates into commercial success.
